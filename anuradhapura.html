Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura, the sacred ancient city, offers a journey through Sri Lanka’s spiritual and cultural heritage, with magnificent stupas, ancient ruins, and serene surroundings.

Back to Destinations

Key Attractions

The sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree, over 2,000 years old, is the oldest documented Bodhi tree in the world, revered by Buddhists and surrounded by beautiful serene grounds perfect for reflection.

The Ruwanwelisaya Stupa impresses visitors with its grand hemispherical structure, intricate architecture, and spiritual significance, forming a cornerstone of the city’s ancient Buddhist heritage.

Jetavanaramaya, one of the tallest brick stupas in the world, showcases the grandeur of ancient construction, with historical importance and panoramic views of the surrounding sacred city.

The twin ponds, Kuttam Pokuna, reveal the sophisticated engineering of ancient civilizations, offering a peaceful and scenic site to appreciate the ingenuity of historic water management systems.

Isurumuniya Temple, renowned for its intricate rock carvings and tranquil surroundings, provides a glimpse into Sri Lanka’s artistic mastery and religious devotion from centuries past.

Things to Do

  • Explore the sacred stupas and temples of Anuradhapura.
  • Walk through the ancient royal palace ruins and historic monuments.
  • Cycle or stroll among serene lakes and Buddhist sites.
  • Witness the daily rituals at sacred Bodhi tree and temples.
  • Learn about Sri Lanka’s cultural and spiritual heritage at local museums.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Anuradhapura is from December to April, during the dry season when the weather is comfortable for sightseeing, temple visits, and exploring the historic ruins.
